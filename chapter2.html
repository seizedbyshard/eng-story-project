The Whispering Fog

Chapter 2: The Maze

Elias ran until his lungs burned, the chilling scrape and moan still echoing, impossibly close, in the suffocating fog. He crashed through what felt like a crumbling fence, tumbling into a dense thicket of overgrown bushes. He lay there, gasping, the adrenaline coursing through him. The sounds seemed to recede, or perhaps the dense foliage muffled them. He pushed himself up, disoriented. The town was a labyrinth of twisted paths and decaying structures, each indistinguishable from the last. Every shadow seemed to writhe, every distant sound a potential threat. He was trapped, utterly lost, and the town itself felt like a living, malevolent entity, breathing around him.
